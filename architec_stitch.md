# 🎯 The Step-by-Step `stitch-loop` Workflow

#### **Step 1: Setup & Initialization (The "Ideation" Phase)**
*   **Action for you:** Install the skill by running `npx skills add google-labs-code/stitch-skills --skill stitch-loop` in your terminal. Give me a description of what we are building.
*   **What we do:** I will analyze your requirements and we will finalize the feature list. I will also read the downloaded `SKILL.md` to load the exact instructions for the `stitch-loop` pattern. 

#### **Step 2: Foundation & Generation (The "Builder" Phase)**
*   **Skill to use:** `stitch-loop` (Generation Mode)
*   **What we do:** Following the skill's instructions, I will act as the **Builder Agent**. I will call Stitch to create a new UI project and use the text-to-screen AI tools to lay down the foundational screens for your application. My goal here is to get the structure and core components onto the canvas quickly.

#### **Step 3: Polish & Refine (The "Reviewer" Phase)**
*   **Skill to use:** `stitch-loop` (Evaluation Mode)
*   **What we do:** Here is where the "baton-passing" happens. I will switch contexts and act as the **Reviewer Agent**. I will analyze the drafted screens from Step 2 against your original prompt. If the design needs polish, I will autonomously use Stitch's editing features to adjust colors, spacing, and layouts to ensure the design meets premium aesthetic standards before we write any code.

#### **Step 4: Code Extraction & Assembly (The "Integrator" Phase)**
*   **Skill to use:** `stitch-loop` (Integration Mode)
*   **What we do:** Once we are perfectly happy with the visual designs in Stitch, I will pass the baton to the **Integrator Agent**. I will export the generated component code from Stitch and systematically assemble it inside your local project directory. I will set up the routing, wire up the pages, and ensure everything runs perfectly on your local development server.
